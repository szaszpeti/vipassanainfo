BUDDHAVIPASSANA.HU




The website was created to help organise meditation courses in Hungary and central/eastern Europe, following the lineage and technique of the Venerable Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo.

Thanat and Kathryn Chindaporn started to teach the technique on the international level more than 25 years ago and they also support organisation of courses in Hungary.



Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo

Highly respected monk and meditation master of our time. He is the abbot of the Buddhist Monastery Wat Chomtong in Northern Thailand. He has practiced and taught Satipattana Vipassana Meditation over 60 years in Thailand as well as Europe and America. The 93 years old most venerable Ajahn is presently residing and teaching in Thailand at Wat Chom Tong.

TEACHERS



THANAT CHINDAPORN

Teacher of Vipassana Meditation in the Therevada Buddhist tradition of Thailand. Since 1985 he was trained under Venerable Ajarn Tong Sirimangalo and the late Venerable Phra Banyat Akkayano. Currently the Director of the International Meditation Department at Wat Chomtong. The past 30 years he has conducted meditation courses in Thailand, Washington State, Hawaii, California, Canada, Mexico, Puerto Rico, Colombia, Germany, Slovenia, Sweden, Spain, France, Israel and India. In 2004 he received Thailand's Benefactor of Buddhism Award for his dissemination of Buddhism world-wide.

KATHRYN CHINDAPORN

Teacher of Vipassana for over 25 years. Since 1988 counducted courses world-wide with his husband Thanat Chindaporn. From 1999 to 2007 she lectured at the Mahachulalongkorn Rajavidyalaya Buddhist University in Chiangmai, Thailand and is currently Thai-English interpreter and Assistant Director of the International Meditation Department at Wat Phradhatu Sri Chomtong Voravihara, Chomtong. Since 1989 she has interpreted for Thai meditation masters, published five Thai-English Buddhist translations. In 2000, Kathryn received the Benefactor of Buddhism Award of Chiangmai Province and the Sema Dhammacakka (Golden Pillar of Dhamma Award) in Recognition of International Dissemination of Buddhism, the latter conferred by HRH Princess Maha Chakri Sirindhorn of Thailand.

ABOUT THE MEDITATION TECHNIQUE



About the technique

The technique that is taught is an intensive form of the burmese Mahasi Sayadaw technique with sequences of mindful prostrations, walking and sitting meditation like it is taught by the
Most Ven. Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo.

* * *
During the course meditators learn through simple and structured exercises to apply mindfulness on body, feelings, mind and mind-objects (Satipatthana – Four
Foundations of Mindfulness) and all arising phenomena are labelled mentally.

* * *
Each participant is guided by the meditation teacher in daily report, during which the exercises are given and explained.

Rules

During the course every student must follow the 8 buddhist precepts:

1.. to abstain from killing any living creature
2. to abstain from taking what is not given
3. to abstain from all sexual activity
4. to abstain from speaking what is not true
5. to abstain from all intoxicants (alcohol, drugs)
6. to abstain from eating solid food after 12 Noon
7. to abstain from sensual entertainment (dancing, singing, reading, listening to the radio), and bodily decoration (jewels, perfume, cosmetics)
8. to abstain from sleeping on high or luxurious beds 



FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS




What is Vipassana?

Vipassana is an ancient meditation technique was rediscovered by Gautama Buddha around 2500 years ago. 
Vipassana means: 'to see things as they really are'.
During the practice one can get deeper insight into the true nature of reality and overcome suffering.

Whom the course is recommended?


The course is recommended for everybody who is willing to follow the teachers instructions and able to keep the rules.
*  *  *
No previous experience is necessary
 *  *  *
Beginner and advanced meditators are also welcome.

What can I gain from practicing?


Buddha was asked: “What have you gained from Meditation?” He replied: “Nothing.”
“However”, Buddha said, “let me tell you what I lost: Anger, Anxiety, Depression, Insecurity, Fear of Old Age and Death.”

Course fees?



Vipassana meditation instructions are offered by the teachers for free. The students will have the opportunity to give voluntary donations at the end of the course to support the teachers expenses (flight ticket, etc.).
* * *
Costs for accomodation & food varies by courses. Information will be given in detail in the course description.

Should I be Buddhist to practice?


No, you don't need to be Buddhist to practice.
* * *
It is important to understand that Vipassana is a meditation technique and not a religion or philosophie. The nature of reality does not depend on religious or philisophical views.

Can I finish the basic course less than 13 days?

No it is not possible to finish the basic course less then 13 days.
* * *
However you are always welcome to practice for shorter period if you wish to. Even some days of practice is beneficial

Courses in the Czech Republic



The center is open 7 days / week and provides teaching and conditions for the practice. The courses are on individual basis, without the need to wait for some date — special courses (as announced in newsletters) and regular meditation groups being an exception to that.
* It is possible to come for introduction: have tea, talk to the teacher and get an idea what is this all about, try the practice for an hour or more;
an arbitrary number of days (one day, or several);
* coming for a part of a day is also fine; basic course with the duration of 21 days;
* it is possible to finish earlier, but you must discuss this with the teacher per e-mail in advance;
* 10-day retreat – for those who have already done the basic course;
* home retreat − for those who have already done the basic course and 10-day retreat, subject to agreement with the teachers;
* weekly evening meditation group, Tuesdays 6pm−8pm, with the possibility of talking to the teacher. Group meeting involves a talk by the teacher, introduction to practice for newcomers and joint meditation.
In principle, we invite anyone to come any time for the practice, but we need to assure good conditions for long-time participants.

Thanks to the generosity of good people, the course can be entirely donation-based this time. We nevertheless hope to cover the costs with your donations. They will be used to cover course expenses: flight tickets for teachers, rental of the house, operating costs. Thanks in advance!
